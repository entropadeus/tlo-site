Overview

Understanding Texas Probate

Probate is the legal process of administering a deceased person's estate, ensuring debts are paid and assets are distributed according to the will or Texas law.

Not all estates require probate, but when it's necessary, having experienced guidance makes the process smoother. Texas offers several probate procedures depending on the size and complexity of the estate.

  • Independent Administration
  • Dependent Administration
  • Muniment of Title
  • Small Estate Affidavits
  • Determination of Heirship
Our Services

Complete Estate Administration

Estate administration involves much more than filing papers with the court. As your attorney, I handle all aspects of the process, from gathering and valuing assets to paying creditors and distributing inheritances to beneficiaries.

My systematic approach ensures nothing is overlooked, helping families avoid costly delays and disputes. I work closely with accountants, appraisers, and financial institutions to complete the administration efficiently.

  • Asset inventory and valuation

Alternatives

Probate Alternatives & Shortcuts

Many families are surprised to learn that formal probate isn't always necessary. Texas law provides several alternatives that can save time and money while still ensuring proper asset transfer to heirs and beneficiaries.

I evaluate each estate to determine if alternatives like small estate affidavits, muniment of title, or trust administration might be appropriate.

  • Small Estate Affidavit (under $75,000)
  • Muniment of Title procedures
  • Joint tenancy and beneficiary transfers
  • Trust administration services
  • Non-probate asset coordination
Dispute Resolution

Will Contests & Estate Disputes

Unfortunately, family disputes sometimes arise during probate proceedings. Whether you need to contest a will due to fraud, undue influence, or lack of capacity, or defend against unfounded challenges, I provide experienced representation.

I work to resolve disputes through negotiation when possible, but I'm fully prepared to litigate when necessary to protect my clients' rights.

  • Will contest litigation
  • Undue influence claims
  • Lack of testamentary capacity
  • Beneficiary disputes
  • Executor misconduct claims
