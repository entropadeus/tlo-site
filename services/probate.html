Understanding Texas Probate Process

Probate is the legal process of administering a deceased person's estate, ensuring debts are paid and assets are distributed according to the will or Texas law. Not all estates require probate, but when it's necessary, having experienced guidance makes the process smoother.

Texas offers several probate procedures depending on the size and complexity of the estate, from simple small estate affidavits to independent administration for larger estates. I help families choose the most efficient path forward.

  • Independent Administration
  • Dependent Administration
  • Muniment of Title
  • Small Estate Affidavits
  • Determination of Heirship

Complete Estate Administration

Estate administration involves much more than filing papers with the court. As your attorney, I handle all aspects of the process, from gathering and valuing assets to paying creditors and distributing inheritances to beneficiaries.

My systematic approach ensures nothing is overlooked, helping families avoid costly delays and disputes. I work closely with accountants, appraisers, and financial institutions to complete the administration efficiently.

  • Asset inventory and valuation
  • Creditor notice and claims processing
  • Final distribution to beneficiaries
  • Court reporting and compliance

Probate Alternatives & Shortcuts

Many families are surprised to learn that formal probate isn't always necessary. Texas law provides several alternatives that can save time and money while still ensuring proper asset transfer to heirs and beneficiaries.

I evaluate each estate to determine if alternatives like small estate affidavits, muniment of title, or trust administration might be appropriate, helping families avoid unnecessary court proceedings when possible.

  • Affidavits of Heirship
  • Small Estate Affidavit (under $75,000)
  • Muniment of Title procedures
  • Joint tenancy and beneficiary transfers
  • Trust administration services
  • Non-probate asset coordination

Executor & Administrator Support

Serving as an executor or administrator is a significant responsibility that comes with legal duties and potential personal liability. I provide comprehensive guidance to executors, helping them fulfill their obligations while avoiding common pitfalls.

From the initial appointment through final distribution, I ensure executors understand their duties, meet all deadlines, and properly document their actions to protect themselves and the estate.

  • Executor appointment and qualification
  • Fiduciary duty guidance
  • Asset protection and management
  • Accounting and reporting requirements
  • Personal liability protection
