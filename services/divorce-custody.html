Divorce & Child Custody

Sometimes relationships end. I provide compassionate guidance through divorce and child custody disputes, helping you secure fair arrangements that protect your children's best interests and your rights as a parent.

Property Division

Fair Property Division

Texas follows community property laws, which means assets acquired during marriage are typically divided equally.

I'll help you understand what you're entitled to and work to ensure a fair division of property, debts, and assets. Separate property owned before marriage, inheritances, and gifts typically remain with the original owner. I'll help identify and protect your separate property interests while ensuring a fair division of community assets.

  • Community property evaluation
  • Separate property protection
  • Debt division strategies
  • Business valuation and division
  • Retirement account division (QDRO)
Child Custody

Child Custody & Conservatorship

Your children's wellbeing is the priority in any custody arrangement. I'll help you understand Texas custody laws and work toward arrangements that serve your children's best interests while protecting your parental rights.

Texas uses the term "conservatorship" instead of custody. Understanding the different types helps you know what to expect and how to protect your relationship with your children.

  • Joint Managing Conservatorship arrangements
  • Standard Possession Order modifications
  • Child support calculations
  • Best interests evaluations
  • Parenting plan development
The Process

The Divorce Process in Texas

Texas allows both fault and no-fault divorce. Most divorces are filed on grounds of "insupportability" (no-fault), but fault grounds may be relevant for property division or custody considerations.

Understanding the legal process can help reduce anxiety during this challenging time. Many divorce cases can be resolved through mediation or collaborative divorce, which can be less costly and emotionally taxing than litigation.

  • Residency requirements evaluation
  • Temporary order applications
  • Mediation and settlement negotiations
  • Discovery and asset evaluation
  • Final decree preparation
Support & Modifications

Support & Ongoing Modifications

Understanding your support obligations or entitlements is crucial for financial planning after divorce. Life changes, and sometimes legal arrangements need to change too. I'm here for the long term.

When circumstances change significantly, court orders may need modification. Job loss or increase in income, relocation, remarriage, changes in children's needs, or safety concerns may warrant modifications to existing orders.

  • Child support calculations and modifications
  • Spousal support (alimony) considerations
  • Custody and visitation modifications
  • Enforcement of court orders
  • Contempt proceedings when necessary
